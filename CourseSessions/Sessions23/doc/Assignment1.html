Answers to Questions

1. How many factors should she select? Why? Why not more or less?

We believe that she should select 7 factors, because

  1. The elbow point on the scree plot become straight after 7 components in the PCA
  2. The eigenvalues are bigger than or equal to 1

2. How should she associate each of the original 29 variables to the factors selected?

She should create a rotated set of factors using a rotation technique such as varimax, then drop all variables for a factor that have a coeffcient less than a certain threshold (we used 0.5).

3. How could she interpret the main factors selected? How should she name each factor by looking at items that were most correlated with it?

For each rotated factor - she should drop all values that have a coefficient less than a certain value (we used 0.5). Then, she needs to look at the set of questions that have a coefficient larger than or equal to 0.5, and infer the type of questions these variables are associated to and label them.

Our Approach

Factor Analysis in 6 steps

  1. Confirm the data in metric

  2. Decide whether to scale or standardize the data

  3. Check the correlation matrix to see if Factor Analysis makes sense

  4. Develop a scree plot and decide on the number of factors to be derived

  5. Interpret the factors (consider factor rotations - technical but useful)

  6. Save factor scores for subsequent analyses
